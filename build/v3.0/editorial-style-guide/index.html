Editorial style guide

Adhering to the Marketing editorial style guide will ensure your publications are produced in a high standard, University-compliant, consistent style.

Language

Australian English (-ise, -our, -er as in adviser, not advisor)

Times

10.00am, 10.30am, 12.00pm, 1.15pm, 3.45pm, 10.00am–4.30pm, 10.00–11.00am (unspaced en dash) or 10–10.30am, 10–11am etc (no double 0; unspaced en dash)

Numbers

  • Spell out one to nine, use digits from 10 onwards (in sentences)
  • Use digits for scores, percentages and measurements
  • per cent (not percent or % in text) % in tables, break out boxes
  • 500, 5000, 50 000, 500 000: spaces in numbers of five digits or more, no commas
  • $25.2 million (not $25.2m)
  • $A, $US
  • 124–127 (unspaced en dash)

Page numbers

  • page 134 (lower case p unless start of sentence)
  • 124–127 (unspaced en dash)

Subject/Majors names

Subject names and major names capitalised (eg ‘The Biology major is...’)

Punctuation

  • No ampersands (excepting when referring to proper names eg Careers & Employment)
  • Dashes: use spaced en dash in body copy

Abbreviations

  • No full points – eg USA, not U.S.A.
  • km/h (kilometres)

Capitalisation

  • Minimal in headings – ‘Leadership and volunteering’
  • Bachelor of Arts (degree name)
  • Graduate School of Humanities and Social Sciences (graduate school name)
  • Faculty of Arts (faculty name)

Hyphenation

  • Use hyphens in compound adjectives, the exception being where the adjective ends in -ly (eg full-time study/decision-making process, but internationally recognised award)
  • No hyphen for corresponding nouns (eg I study full time/decision making rests with management)
  • No hanging hyphens (eg full and part-time study, not full- and part-time study)

Apostrophes

  • “Double” apostrophes for direct speech
  • ‘Single’ apostrophes for everything but direct speech

Bulleted lists

  • Preceded with a colon
  • No punctuation until after the last point
  • No semicolons
  • Capital at start of each

Dates and years

  • 8 August 2011
  • 5–15 August 2011 (unspaced en dash)
  • 1990s
  • Semester 1, 2010
  • Sunday 15 August 2010
  • 5 November 2010

Serial commas

Use serial commas in lists – ie before ‘and’ – when required for clarity (eg Editorial English, Structural Editing, and Research Methodology). But avoid use when unnecessary for clarity (eg seminars, lectures and tutorials)

Phone numbers

  • +61 3 8344 4000
  • Phone: +61 3 8344 4000
  • Fax: +61 3 8344 5100
  • Mobile: +61 4xx xxx xxx
  • Exclude fax numbers from contact details unless absolutely necessary (eg international admissions)
  • 13 MELB (13 6352) ie use both alpha and numerics, the latter in brackets

Web addresses

  • Use www. (not http://) unless link does not work without, or does not include ‘www’.
  • If sentence precedes the web address, follow with colon, then start web address on new line.

Qualifications

Example: BA Melb. BSc (Hons) Monash MA La Trobe PhD Melb. FAICD FCA FCPA (ie: qualifications in ascending order, followed by honorifics and professional association memberships unitalicised)
